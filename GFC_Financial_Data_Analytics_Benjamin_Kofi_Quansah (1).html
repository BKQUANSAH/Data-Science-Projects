Project with Global Finance Corp. (GFC), focusing on developing an AI-powered chatbot to revolutionize their financial data analysis.

Project context:

Our goal is to extract meaningful insights from 10-K financial reports. These insights will feed into the AI chatbot, enabling it to provide in-depth financial performance analysis.

Objective

Extract key financial data for the last three fiscal years from the 10-K filings of Microsoft, Tesla, and Apple. Use Python to analyze this data, focusing on trends and insights that could inform the development of an AI-powered financial chatbot.

The following financial figures are to be extracted and used for the analysis: Total Revenue, Net Income, Total Assets, Total Liabilities, and Cash Flow from Operating Activities.

Data Description

The data contains Total Revenue, Net Income, Total Assets, Total Liabilities, and Cash Flow from Operating Activities.

Data Dictionary

  • EIN: Unique Employer ID of the company with the IRS
  • Company_Name: Name of the company
  • Year: Year for each company For each company, the financial data for the last three fiscal years.
  • Total_Revenue: Total Revenue for each company for the last three fiscal years
  • Net_Income: Net Income for each company for the last three fiscal years
  • Total_Assets: Total Assets for each company for the last three fiscal years
  • Total_Liabilities: Total Liabilities for each company for the last three fiscal years
  • Cash Flow from Operating Activities: Cash Flow from Operating Activities for each company for the last three fiscal years

# Calculating growth percentages and adding them as new columns

# Function to calculate year-over-year growth percentage
def calculate_growth_percentage(df, column):
  """Calculates year-over-year growth percentage for a given column."""
  df[column + ' Growth %'] = df.groupby('Company_Name')[column].pct_change() * 100
  return df


# Applying the function to each financial metric
for column in ['Total_Revenue', 'Net_Income', 'Total_Assets', 'Total_Liabilities', 'Cash Flow_Operating Activities']:
  data = calculate_growth_percentage(data, column)

# Calculate the growth trends using the new columns
growth_trends = data.groupby('Company_Name').agg(
    revenue_growth=('Total_Revenue Growth %', 'mean'),
    profit_growth=('Net_Income Growth %', 'mean'),
    asset_growth=('Total_Assets Growth %', 'mean'),
    liabilities_growth=('Total_Liabilities Growth %', 'mean'),
    # Change the column name to match the one created in calculate_growth_percentage function
    cash_flow_growth=('Cash Flow_Operating Activities Growth %', 'mean')
)

# Displaying the growth trends
print("Growth Trend Over 3 Fiscal Years for Each Company:")
print(growth_trends)

print("\nGrowth Trend (Transposed):")
print(growth_trends.T)

Summary Findings

Revenue Growth:

  • Revenue growth was strongest for Microsoft, averaging 11.28% over the three years. This would imply that the market is well-expanding and that there is successful offering of product/service offerings.
  • Tesla showed really impressive revenue growth, averaging 9.87%, driven by continuous demand for electric vehicles and other energy solutions.
  • Apple represented a slight decrease in revenue, with an average growth of -0.39% average. This may signify that saturation is beginning to set in, or there is increased competition in the core product segments.

Profit Growth:

  • Microsoft again leads the way, with an impressive average profit growth of 10.64%, reflecting operational efficiency and the capability to translate revenues into earnings.
  • Apple experienced an average profit growth of -3.09% probably due to increased production costs or price pressures
  • Tesla's profit growth drastically fell on average by -16.63%, probably because of investment in research and development, expansion, or competitive pressures.

Asset Growth:

  • The maximum asset growth stood by Tesla at 21.99% due to immense investments in manufacturing capacity, infrastructure, and new technologies..
  • Microsoft also showed impressive asset growth of 18.62% average; this would most likely result from acquisitions, expansion efforts, and heavy cloud infrastructure investments.
  • Apple had an asset growth moderated at 1.73 percent, which indicated that Apple has been more conservative in terms of capital expenditure versus the other two companies.

Liabilities Growth:

  • Tesla showed the biggest growth of liabilities by 15.27%, probably due to debt financing which was necessary for such rapid expansion.
  • Microsoft also tends to increase its average liabilities by 11.10%, reflecting strategic investments and acquisitions.
  • Apple has comparative low growth of liabilities at 1.10%, probably a manifestation of the far more conservative attitude toward debt financing.

Cash Flow from Operating Activities Growth:

  • Microsoft's cash flow from operating activities increased the most, at 16.86%, reflecting healthy cash generation from core operations.
  • Tesla experienced modest growth in cash flow from operating activities at 1.30%, suggesting potential challenges in translating revenue growth into consistent cash flow.
  • Apple faced a decline in cash flow from operating activities growth, averaging -1.26%, which could be due to factors like changes in working capital or investment cycles.

Overall Insights:

  • Microsoft saw the most steady and strongest growth across all the financial metrics, possibly indicative of good competitive positioning and efficient management.
  • Tesla revenues grew high, asset growth was high too, whereas profitability and generation of cash flows were poor items raising some potential risks with the rapid expansion.
  • Apple had relatively low growth in general or declined in some fields, what indeed should raise some concern from market saturation through to competitive pressure.

Methodology, Observations, and Conclusions

Methodology

  • The 10-K Financial data for 3 fiscal years (2022,2023, and 2024) were examined and five data points were selected for the data analysis. These data points (metrics) were Total Revenue, Net Income, Total Assets, Total Liabilities, and Cash Flow from Operating Activities.
  • The data extracted from these financial statements were put into an excel spreadsheet, which was subsequently converted into a Comma Seperated Value(CSV) file for easier handling and then loaded into a dataframe.
  • The objective for performing the analysis was provided, including the data description and data dictionary.
  • The necessary files were imported to help with data manipulation and visualization, as well as loading the dataset to help with the analysis.
  • The data was checked for missing and duplicated values to ensure its readiness and integrity for analysis.
  • Year was converted into an object or categorical variable.

Observations

  • The data provided shows the growth trend over three fiscal years for Apple, Microsoft, and Tesla across several financial metrics: revenue growth, profit growth, asset growth, liabilities growth, and cash flow growth.

  • The transposed table makes it easier to compare the companies side-by-side for each financial metric. These have been highlighted in the summary findings.

  • There is a strong correlation among the financial metrics as can be seen from the heatmap.

Conclusions

  • Microsoft is the strongest performer, demonstrating healthy financial growth across all financial metrics.
  • Tesla shows high revenue and asset growth but struggles with profitability and a rising liability burden. This suggests a growth-focused strategy that may be sacrificing profits in the short term.
  • Apple shows concerning trends with declining revenue, profit, and cash flow. These issues need to be addressed to ensure future growth.
  • Growth rates can be compared to industry averages to see how these firms are performing against their competitors.
